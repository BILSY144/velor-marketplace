# Velor Global Marketing & Email Strategy

Status: adopted 7 July 2026. Owner: William (director) / AI operations.
Buyer launch date: **6 August 2026**. Everything before that date serves one goal: sellers signed up and listing. Everything after it serves one goal: buyers knowing Velor exists and trusting it.

---

## The two-phase plan

| Phase | Window | Single goal | Primary metric |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------------|
| 1. Seller recruitment | 7 Jul - 5 Aug | Sellers joined AND listing products | Active sellers with 3+ live listings |
| 2. Buyer launch | 6 Aug onward | Buyers discover and trust Velor | First orders, D7 retention, CAC by channel |

The seller pitch writes the buyer pitch: every seller recruited before 6 August makes the marketplace worth visiting on 6 August.

---

## Phase 1 — Seller recruitment (now to 5 Aug)

### Positioning to sellers
- "Be live before the buyers arrive": founding-seller framing with the 6 August buyer launch as genuine, honest urgency.
- Free to start (Starter tier, 20 listings, no listing fees), keep 85%+ of every sale, paid weekly after delivery confirmation.
- Global by default: one listing reaches buyers in nearly every country, with live currency conversion and customs paperwork guidance built in.
- For artisans and makers specifically: maker story on every listing, origin-country flags, and a marketplace explicitly built to showcase authentic cultural products rather than bury them under mass-produced catalogue (see velor-cultural-marketplace vision).

### Seller segments and channels
1. **Artisans, family workshops, cooperatives** (the differentiator): partner-first outreach through artisan cooperatives, fair-trade organisations and export-promotion bodies rather than one-by-one cold email; WhatsApp-friendly first contact where possible; do not assume high digital literacy or English.
2. **Independent brands already selling online** (Etsy, Instagram, TikTok Shop sellers): direct outreach via the existing scout-sellers -> outreach-auto pipeline (SellerProspect scoring 7+, 3-email sequence day 0/3/7, hard cap 3 emails per seller, unsubscribe honoured).
3. **Established SMB wholesalers/brands**: formal partnership-tone outreach; Enterprise tier pitch (0% commission, API access, account manager).

### Email programme (seller side)
- Sending identity: agent emails from customerservice@velorcommerce.co.uk (established Resend domain); seller support replies via sellers@velorcommerce.store. Verify SPF/DKIM/DMARC on both domains before any volume increase, and ramp send volume gradually (deliverability warm-up) rather than bursting.
- Sequences: (a) cold outreach 3-step; (b) application received -> approved -> first-listing nudge onboarding drip; (c) "listed but no sales yet" coaching email; (d) weekly founding-seller update until launch (what's new, seller count, launch countdown).
- Every email personalised (name, platform, product category); never bulk-identical; log every send in OutreachLog. These rules already exist in velor-agents and are non-negotiable.
- Gate: OUTREACH_ENABLED stays off until William confirms the site is presentable; flipping it on is the Phase 1 go signal.

### Phase 1 working targets (targets, not promises)
- 100+ approved sellers by 6 August, of which 40+ with 3 or more live listings.
- 1,000+ live listings across at least 10 categories and 15+ origin countries.
- Application-to-first-listing time under 7 days (measure and remove friction weekly).

## Phase 2 — Buyer launch (6 Aug onward)

### Launch story (PR and organic)
Two angles, used together:
1. **"Shop the world"** - buy authentic products directly from real makers and independent brands in dozens of countries, with your money protected until delivery. Emotional, product-led, visual.
2. **"The first fully AI-run marketplace"** - a genuinely novel tech story for press, newsletters, and the AI-curious audience. Factual and demonstrable.

### Paid channels (per velor-agents Agent 5 targets)
- Meta ads: target CAC under 8 GBP. TikTok: under 6 GBP. Google Shopping: under 10 GBP. Email: under 2 GBP.
- Creative testing rule: 3 days at 5 GBP/day per creative before scaling; never scale below 2x ROAS; kill losers fast.
- First campaigns: buyer-protection-led ("your money is held until you confirm delivery") and origin-led ("jewellery from Japan, textiles from India, ceramics from Portugal") creative sets, A/B against each other.

### Organic channels
- SEO: category pages and origin-country pages are the long-term asset; every product listing carries real seller, origin and materials data that generic dropship stores cannot match.
- Content calendar (Agent 5 baseline): short-form video led - maker stories and product-origin stories are natively strong TikTok/Reels material; 2 TikTok/day, 1 Instagram post/day, 3 stories/day, 1 blog/week once staffed by the agent pipeline.
- Social proof: live page-view counter already on the homepage; add seller-count and country-count once the numbers are impressive rather than before.

### Email programme (buyer side)
- Pre-launch: the existing email capture popup becomes a launch waitlist ("First access 6 August"); one warm-up email the week before launch, one launch-day email.
- Post-launch: welcome series (protection story, how it works, featured makers), browse-abandon and cart-abandon flows, weekly "new from the world" digest segmented by category interest.
- Frequency cap: no more than 2 marketing emails per week per buyer; every email has one clear job.

### Localisation sequencing
Already live: 20-currency switcher, live FX at checkout, global shipping destinations, origin flags. Next in order of impact: (1) translated seller outreach templates for top recruiting corridors, (2) translated buyer landing pages for top 5 non-English buyer markets once paid data shows which they are, (3) full site i18n only when justified by traffic.

## Measurement (Agent 9 daily KPIs)
Seller side: applications/week, approval rate, time-to-first-listing, active sellers, listings count, listings per seller.
Buyer side: sessions, conversion rate, CAC per channel, ROAS, D1/D7/D30 retention, first-order rate from waitlist.
Trust: crash-free sessions, dispute rate, on-time delivery rate, review scores.
All surfaced in the daily director briefing to willsinclair144@gmail.com.

## Hard rules carried over (do not violate for growth)
- Never fabricate numbers in marketing copy (no "millions of buyers" until true).
- Max 3 outreach emails per seller, ever. Unsubscribes honoured immediately.
- No fake urgency, fake reviews, or fake social proof - the DMCC Act (UK) actively polices fake reviews and the whole brand rests on trust.
- UK ASA/CAP rules apply to all ads; substantiate every claim (see velor-advertising skill for creative standards).
